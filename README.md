<h2> Timeline of European exploration</h2>

<h2><span id="15th_century" class="mw-headline">15th century</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">&nbsp;</div>
<ul>
<li>1418 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Kingdom of Portugal" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Portugal">Portuguese</a>&nbsp;explorers&nbsp;<a title="Jo&atilde;o Gon&ccedil;alves Zarco" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Gon%C3%A7alves_Zarco">Jo&atilde;o Gon&ccedil;alves Zarco</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Trist&atilde;o Vaz Teixeira" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trist%C3%A3o_Vaz_Teixeira">Trist&atilde;o Vaz Teixeira</a>&nbsp;discover&nbsp;<a title="Porto Santo Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porto_Santo_Island">Porto Santo Island</a>&nbsp;in the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Madeira Islands" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira_Islands">Madeira archipelago</a>.</li>
<li>1419 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Jo&atilde;o Gon&ccedil;alves Zarco" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Gon%C3%A7alves_Zarco">Gon&ccedil;alves</a>&nbsp;and Vaz discover the main island of&nbsp;<a title="Madeira Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira_Island">Madeira</a>.</li>
<li>1431 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Diogo de Silves" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diogo_de_Silves">Diogo de Silves</a>&nbsp;discovers the&nbsp;<a title="Azores" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azores">Azores</a>.</li>
<li>1434 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Gil Eanes" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gil_Eanes">Gil Eanes</a>&nbsp;passes&nbsp;<a title="Cape Chaunar" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Chaunar">Cabo de N&atilde;o</a>&nbsp;and becomes the first to sail beyond&nbsp;<a title="Cape Bojador" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Bojador">Cape Bojador</a>&nbsp;and return alive.</li>
<li>1444 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Dinis Dias" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dinis_Dias">Dinis Dias</a>&nbsp;reaches the mouth of the&nbsp;<a title="Senegal River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senegal_River">Senegal River</a>.</li>
<li>1446 &ndash; The Portuguese reach the mainland peninsula of&nbsp;<a title="Cap-Vert" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cap-Vert">Cape Verde</a>&nbsp;and the&nbsp;<a title="Gambia River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gambia_River">Gambia River</a>.</li>
<li>1456 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Alvise Cadamosto" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alvise_Cadamosto">Alvise Cadamosto</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Diogo Gomes" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diogo_Gomes">Diogo Gomes</a>&nbsp;explore the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Cape Verde Islands" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands">Cape Verde Islands</a>, 560 kilometres (350&nbsp;mi) west of the Cape Verde peninsula.</li>
<li>1460 &ndash;&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="P&ecirc;ro de Sintra" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P%C3%AAro_de_Sintra">P&ecirc;ro de Sintra</a>&nbsp;reaches&nbsp;<a title="Sierra Leone" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Leone">Sierra Leone</a>.</li>
<li>1470 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Cape Palmas" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Palmas">Cape Palmas</a>&nbsp;is passed.</li>
<li>1472 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Fern&atilde;o do P&oacute;" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fern%C3%A3o_do_P%C3%B3">Fern&atilde;o do P&oacute;</a>&nbsp;discovers the island of&nbsp;<a title="Bioko" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioko">Bioko</a>.</li>
<li>1473 &ndash;&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Lopo Gon&ccedil;alves" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lopo_Gon%C3%A7alves">Lopo Gon&ccedil;alves</a>&nbsp;is the first European sailor to cross the&nbsp;<a title="Equator" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator">Equator</a>.</li>
<li>1474&ndash;75 &ndash; Ruy de Sequeira discovers&nbsp;<a title="S&atilde;o Tom&eacute; and Pr&iacute;ncipe" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe">S&atilde;o Tom&eacute; and Pr&iacute;ncipe</a>.</li>
<li>1482 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Diogo C&atilde;o" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diogo_C%C3%A3o">Diogo C&atilde;o</a>&nbsp;reaches the&nbsp;<a title="Congo River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congo_River">Congo River</a>, where he erects a&nbsp;<em>padr&atilde;o</em>&nbsp;("pillar of stone").</li>
<li>1485&ndash;86 &ndash; C&atilde;o reaches&nbsp;<a title="Cape Cross" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Cross">Cape Cross</a>, where he erects his last&nbsp;<em>padr&atilde;o</em>.</li>
<li>1487&ndash;92 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="P&ecirc;ro da Covilh&atilde;" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P%C3%AAro_da_Covilh%C3%A3">P&ecirc;ro da Covilh&atilde;</a>&nbsp;travels to&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Arabia" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabia">Arabia</a>, to the mouth of the&nbsp;<a title="Red Sea" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea">Red Sea</a>, and then eastward by sail to the&nbsp;<a title="Malabar Coast" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malabar_Coast">Malabar Coast</a>&nbsp;(visiting&nbsp;<a title="Kozhikode" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kozhikode">Calicut</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Goa" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goa">Goa</a>&nbsp;on the&nbsp;<a title="India" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India">Indian</a>&nbsp;subcontinent). He later sails south along the east coast of Africa, visiting the trading stations of&nbsp;<a title="Mombasa" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mombasa">Mombasa</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Zanzibar" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Sofala" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sofala">Sofala</a>; on his return journey he visits&nbsp;<a title="Mecca" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecca">Mecca</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Medina" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medina">Medina</a>&nbsp;before reaching&nbsp;<a title="Ethiopia" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>&nbsp;in search of the mythical&nbsp;<a title="Prester John" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prester_John">Prester John</a>.</li>
<li>1488 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Bartolomeu Dias" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bartolomeu_Dias">Bartolomeu Dias</a>&nbsp;rounds the "Cape of Storms" (<a title="Cape of Good Hope" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a>), at the southernmost tip of the African continent.</li>
<li>1492 &ndash; Under the patronage of the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic Monarchs" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<a title="Spain" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain">Spain</a>, Italian explorer&nbsp;<a title="Christopher Columbus" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a>&nbsp;discovers the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Bahamas" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bahamas">Bahamas</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Cuba" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuba">Cuba</a>, and "Espa&ntilde;ola" (<a title="Hispaniola" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>), which are only later recognized as part of the&nbsp;<a title="New World" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_World">New World</a>.</li>
<li>1493&ndash;94 &ndash; On his second voyage to the&nbsp;<a title="Americas" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americas">Americas</a>, Columbus discovers&nbsp;<a title="Dominica" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominica">Dominica</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Guadeloupe" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a>, among other islands of the&nbsp;<a title="Lesser Antilles" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesser_Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, as well as&nbsp;<a title="Puerto Rico" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puerto_Rico">Puerto Rico</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Jamaica" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamaica">Jamaica</a>.</li>
<li>1497 &ndash; Under the commission of&nbsp;<a title="Henry VII of England" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_VII_of_England">Henry VII of England</a>, Italian explorer&nbsp;<a title="John Cabot" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Cabot">John Cabot</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Newfoundland (island)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)">Newfoundland</a>, becoming the first European to explore the coast of mainland&nbsp;<a title="North America" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America">North America</a>&nbsp;since the Norse explorations of&nbsp;<a title="Vinland" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinland">Vinland</a>&nbsp;five centuries earlier.</li>
<li>1497&ndash;98 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Vasco da Gama" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama">Vasco da Gama</a>&nbsp;sails to&nbsp;<a title="India" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India">India</a>&nbsp;and back.</li>
<li>1498 &ndash; On his third voyage to the Americas, Christopher Columbus discovers mainland&nbsp;<a title="South America" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_America">South America</a>.</li>
<li>1499 &ndash; Spanish explorer&nbsp;<a title="Alonso de Ojeda" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alonso_de_Ojeda">Alonso de Ojeda</a>&nbsp;explores the South American mainland from about&nbsp;<a title="Cayenne" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cayenne">Cayenne</a>&nbsp;(in modern&nbsp;<a title="French Guiana" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Guiana">French Guiana</a>) to&nbsp;<a title="Cabo de la Vela" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabo_de_la_Vela">Cabo de la Vela</a>&nbsp;(in modern&nbsp;<a title="Colombia" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombia">Colombia</a>), discovering the mouth of the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Orinoco River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orinoco_River">Orinoco River</a>&nbsp;and entering&nbsp;<a title="Lake Maracaibo" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Maracaibo">Lake Maracaibo</a>.</li>
<li>1499 &ndash; Italian explorer&nbsp;<a title="Amerigo Vespucci" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amerigo_Vespucci">Amerigo Vespucci</a>&nbsp;discovers the mouth of the&nbsp;<a title="Amazon River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_River">Amazon River</a>&nbsp;and reaches 6&deg;S latitude, in present-day northern&nbsp;<a title="Brazil" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil">Brazil</a>.</li>
<li>1499 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Jo&atilde;o Fernandes Lavrador" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Fernandes_Lavrador">Jo&atilde;o Fernandes&nbsp;<em>Lavrador</em></a>, together with&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="P&ecirc;ro de Barcelos" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P%C3%AAro_de_Barcelos">P&ecirc;ro de Barcelos</a>, sight&nbsp;<a title="Labrador" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labrador">Labrador</a>.</li>
<li>1499 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Gaspar Corte-Real" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaspar_Corte-Real">Gaspar</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Miguel Corte-Real" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_Corte-Real">Miguel Corte-Real</a>&nbsp;reach and map&nbsp;<a title="Greenland" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenland">Greenland</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span id="16th_century" class="mw-headline">16th century</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">&nbsp;</div>
<div class="thumb tright">&nbsp;</div>
<ul>
<li>1500 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Vicente Y&aacute;&ntilde;ez Pinz&oacute;n" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vicente_Y%C3%A1%C3%B1ez_Pinz%C3%B3n">Vicente Y&aacute;&ntilde;ez Pinz&oacute;n</a>&nbsp;reaches the northeast coast of what today is Brazil at a cape he names "Santa Maria de la Consolaci&oacute;n" (<a title="Cabo de Santo Agostinho" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabo_de_Santo_Agostinho">Cabo de Santo Agostinho</a>) and sails fifty miles up a river he names the "Mara&ntilde;&oacute;n" (<a title="Amazon River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_River">Amazon</a>).</li>
<li>1500 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Pedro &Aacute;lvares Cabral" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedro_%C3%81lvares_Cabral">Pedro &Aacute;lvares Cabral</a>&nbsp;makes the "official" discovery of Brazil,&nbsp;leading the first&nbsp;<a title="2nd Portuguese India Armada (Cabral, 1500)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2nd_Portuguese_India_Armada_(Cabral,_1500)">expedition</a>&nbsp;that united Europe, America, Africa, and Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"></sup></li>
<li>1500 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Jo&atilde;o Fernandes Lavrador" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_Fernandes_Lavrador">Jo&atilde;o Fernandes</a>&nbsp;reaches&nbsp;<a title="Cape Farewell, Greenland" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Farewell,_Greenland">Cape Farewell</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Greenland" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenland">Greenland</a>&nbsp;("Terra do Lavrador", or Land of the Husbandman).</li>
<li>1500&ndash;02 &ndash; Gaspar and Miguel Corte Real discover and name the coasts of "Terra Verde" (likely Newfoundland) and Labrador.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_10-1" class="reference"></sup></li>
<li>1500&ndash;01 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Diogo Dias" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diogo_Dias">Diogo Dias</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Madagascar" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar">Madagascar</a>&nbsp;and reaches the gate of the&nbsp;<a title="Red Sea" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea">Red Sea</a>, the&nbsp;<a title="Bab-el-Mandeb" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bab-el-Mandeb">Bab-el-Mandeb</a>&nbsp;Strait.</li>
<li>1500 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Rodrigo de Bastidas" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodrigo_de_Bastidas">Rodrigo de Bastidas</a>&nbsp;explores the Colombian coast from Cabo de la Vela to the&nbsp;<a title="Gulf of Urab&aacute;" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Urab%C3%A1">Gulf of Urab&aacute;</a>.</li>
<li>1501&ndash;02 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Gon&ccedil;alo Coelho" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gon%C3%A7alo_Coelho">Gon&ccedil;alo Coelho</a>&nbsp;discovers "Rio de Janeiro" (<a title="Guanabara Bay" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guanabara_Bay">Guanabara Bay</a>).</li>
<li>1502&ndash;03 &ndash; On his fourth voyage to the Americas, Christopher Columbus explores the North American mainland from&nbsp;<a title="Guanaja" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guanaja">Guanaja</a>&nbsp;off modern&nbsp;<a title="Honduras" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honduras">Honduras</a>&nbsp;to the present-day border of&nbsp;<a title="Panama" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panama">Panama</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Colombia" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombia">Colombia</a>.</li>
<li>1505 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Juan de Berm&uacute;dez" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_de_Berm%C3%BAdez">Juan de Berm&uacute;dez</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Bermuda" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.</li>
<li>1506 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Louren&ccedil;o de Almeida" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louren%C3%A7o_de_Almeida">Louren&ccedil;o de Almeida</a>&nbsp;reaches the&nbsp;<a title="Maldives" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maldives">Maldives</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Sri Lanka" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.</li>
<li>1506 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Trist&atilde;o da Cunha" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trist%C3%A3o_da_Cunha">Trist&atilde;o da Cunha</a>&nbsp;discovers the remote island of&nbsp;<a title="Tristan da Cunha" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tristan_da_Cunha">Tristan da Cunha</a>&nbsp;in the South Atlantic Ocean.</li>
<li>1509 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Diogo Lopes de Sequeira" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diogo_Lopes_de_Sequeira">Diogo Lopes de Sequeira</a>&nbsp;reaches&nbsp;<a title="Sumatra" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumatra">Sumatra</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Malacca" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malacca">Malacca</a>.</li>
<li>1511 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Duarte Fernandes" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duarte_Fernandes">Duarte Fernandes</a>&nbsp;leads a diplomatic mission to&nbsp;<a title="Ayutthaya Kingdom" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ayutthaya_Kingdom">Ayutthaya Kingdom</a>&nbsp;(Siam or&nbsp;<a title="Thailand" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand">Thailand</a>).</li>
<li>1511 &ndash; Rui Nunes da Cunha leads a diplomatic mission to&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Pegu" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pegu">Pegu</a>&nbsp;(Burma or&nbsp;<a title="Myanmar" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar">Myanmar</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Galvano_16-0" class="reference"></sup></li>
<li>1511&ndash;12 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Jo&atilde;o de Lisboa" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jo%C3%A3o_de_Lisboa">Jo&atilde;o de Lisboa</a>&nbsp;and Estev&atilde;o de Fr&oacute;is discover the "Cape of Santa Maria" (<a class="mw-redirect" title="Punta Del Este" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punta_Del_Este">Punta Del Este</a>) in the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Rio de La Plata" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rio_de_La_Plata">River Plate</a>, exploring its estuary, and traveling as far south as the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Gulf of San Matias" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_San_Matias">Gulf of San Matias</a>&nbsp;at 42&ordm;S, in present-day&nbsp;<a title="Uruguay" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uruguay">Uruguay</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Argentina" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argentina">Argentina</a>&nbsp;(penetrating 300&nbsp;km (186&nbsp;mi) "around the Gulf").<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"></sup></li>
<li>1511&ndash;12 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Ant&oacute;nio de Abreu" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Abreu">Ant&oacute;nio de Abreu</a>&nbsp;sails through the&nbsp;<a title="Strait of Malacca" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Malacca">Strait of Malacca</a>, between Sumatra and&nbsp;<a title="Bangka Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangka_Island">Bangka</a>, and along the coasts of&nbsp;<a title="Java" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Java">Java</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Bali" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bali">Bali</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Lombok" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lombok">Lombok</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Sumbawa" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumbawa">Sumbawa</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Flores" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flores">Flores</a>&nbsp;to the "Spice Islands" (<a title="Maluku Islands" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maluku_Islands">Maluku</a>).</li>
<li>1513 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Jorge &Aacute;lvares" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jorge_%C3%81lvares">Jorge &Aacute;lvares</a>&nbsp;becomes the first European to reach&nbsp;<a title="China" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China">China</a>&nbsp;by sea, landing on&nbsp;<a title="Nei Lingding Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nei_Lingding_Island">Nei Lingding Island</a>&nbsp;at the&nbsp;<a title="Pearl River Delta" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pearl_River_Delta">Pearl River Delta</a>.</li>
<li>1513 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Vasco N&uacute;&ntilde;ez de Balboa" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasco_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_de_Balboa">Vasco N&uacute;&ntilde;ez de Balboa</a>&nbsp;crosses the&nbsp;<a title="Isthmus of Panama" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a>&nbsp;and reaches the&nbsp;<a title="Bay of San Miguel" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_San_Miguel">Bay of San Miguel</a>, discovering the "Mar del Sur" (<a title="Pacific Ocean" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>).</li>
<li>1513 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Juan Ponce de Le&oacute;n" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Ponce_de_Le%C3%B3n">Juan Ponce de Le&oacute;n</a>&nbsp;discovers "La Florida" (<a title="Florida" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida">Florida</a>) and the&nbsp;<a title="Yucat&aacute;n Peninsula" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula">Yucat&aacute;n</a>.</li>
<li>1514&ndash;15 &ndash; Ant&oacute;nio Fernandes reaches present-day&nbsp;<a title="Zimbabwe" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>.</li>
<li>1515 &ndash; Gonzalo de Badajoz crosses the Isthmus of Panama at the site of&nbsp;<a title="Nombre de Dios, Col&oacute;n" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nombre_de_Dios,_Col%C3%B3n">Nombre de Dios</a>, reaching as far as the interior of the&nbsp;<a title="Azuero Peninsula" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azuero_Peninsula">Azuero Peninsula</a>.</li>
<li>1516 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Juan D&iacute;az de Sol&iacute;s" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_D%C3%ADaz_de_Sol%C3%ADs">Juan D&iacute;az de Sol&iacute;s</a>&nbsp;explores the River Plate estuary and names it "La Mar Dulce" ("The Fresh-Water Sea").</li>
<li>1516 &ndash; Portuguese traders land in&nbsp;<a title="Da Nang" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Da_Nang">Da Nang</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Champa" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Champa">Champa</a>, naming it&nbsp;<a title="Cochinchina" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cochinchina">Cochinchina</a>&nbsp;(modern&nbsp;<a title="Vietnam" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam">Vietnam</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Yule_23-0" class="reference"></sup></li>

<div class="thumb tright">&nbsp;</div>
<ul>

<li>1792&ndash;93 &ndash; Mackenzie ascends the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Peace River (Canada)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_River_(Canada)">Peace</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Parsnip River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parsnip_River">Parsnip</a>, crosses the&nbsp;<a title="Canadian Rockies" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian_Rockies">Canadian Rockies</a>&nbsp;to the headwaters of the&nbsp;<a title="Fraser River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fraser_River">Fraser</a>, ascends the&nbsp;<a title="West Road River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Road_River">West Road River</a>&nbsp;and crosses the&nbsp;<a title="Coast Mountains" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coast_Mountains">Coast Mountains</a>, reaching the&nbsp;<a title="Bella Coola River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bella_Coola_River">Bella Coola</a>, which he descends to&nbsp;<a title="North Bentinck Arm" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Bentinck_Arm">North Bentinck Arm</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Dean Channel" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dean_Channel">Dean Channel</a>.</li>
<li>1796 &ndash; Scottish explorer&nbsp;<a title="Mungo Park (explorer)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mungo_Park_(explorer)">Mungo Park</a>&nbsp;reaches the upper&nbsp;<a title="Niger River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger_River">Niger</a>, exploring it from&nbsp;<a title="S&eacute;gou" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A9gou">S&eacute;gou</a>&nbsp;to&nbsp;<a title="Silla, Mali" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silla,_Mali">Silla</a>.</li>
<li>1797&ndash;98 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="George Bass" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Bass">George Bass</a>&nbsp;explores from Cape Howe to&nbsp;<a title="Western Port" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Port">Western Port</a>, discovering the&nbsp;<a title="Bass Strait" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bass_Strait">Bass Strait</a>.</li>
<li>1798 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="John Fearn (whaler)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Fearn_(whaler)">John Fearn</a>&nbsp;discovers "Pleasant Island" (<a title="Nauru" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nauru">Nauru</a>).</li>
<li>1798 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Francisco de Lacerda" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_de_Lacerda">Francisco de Lacerda</a>&nbsp;travels from&nbsp;<a title="Tete, Mozambique" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tete,_Mozambique">Tete</a>&nbsp;northwest to&nbsp;<a title="Lake Mweru" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Mweru">Lake Mweru</a>.</li>
<li>1798&ndash;99 &ndash; English cartographer&nbsp;<a title="Matthew Flinders" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matthew_Flinders">Matthew Flinders</a>&nbsp;and George Bass circumnavigate&nbsp;<a title="Tasmania" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tasmania">Tasmania</a>, proving its insularity.</li>
</ul>

<li>1800 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="James Grant (navigator)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Grant_(navigator)">James Grant</a>&nbsp;discovers the Australian coastline from&nbsp;<a title="Cape Banks" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Banks">Cape Banks</a>&nbsp;to&nbsp;<a title="Cape Otway" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Otway">Cape Otway</a>.</li>

<li>1802 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="John Murray (Australian explorer)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Murray_(Australian_explorer)">John Murray</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Port Phillip Bay" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_Phillip_Bay">Port Phillip Bay</a>.</li>

<li>1806 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Abraham Bristow" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abraham_Bristow">Abraham Bristow</a>&nbsp;discovers the&nbsp;<a title="Auckland Islands" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auckland_Islands">Auckland Islands</a>.</li>
<li>1808 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Simon Fraser (explorer)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simon_Fraser_(explorer)">Simon Fraser</a>&nbsp;descends the&nbsp;<a title="Fraser River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fraser_River">Fraser River</a>&nbsp;for some 800 kilometres (500&nbsp;mi) to its mouth, reaching the Strait of Georgia.</li>
<li>1810 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Frederick Hasselborough" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Hasselborough">Frederick Hasselborough</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Campbell Island, New Zealand" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campbell_Island,_New_Zealand">Campbell</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Macquarie Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macquarie_Island">Macquarie Islands</a>.</li>
<li>1811&ndash;12 &ndash;&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="W. Price Hunt" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W._Price_Hunt">Wilson Price Hunt</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Union Pass" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_Pass">Union Pass</a>&nbsp;in the&nbsp;<a title="Wind River Range" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wind_River_Range">Wind River Range</a>&nbsp;and reaches the upper Snake River, while&nbsp;<a title="Robert Stuart (explorer)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Stuart_(explorer)">Robert Stuart</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="South Pass (Wyoming)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Pass_(Wyoming)">South Pass</a>&mdash;his route would later become the&nbsp;<a title="Oregon Trail" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oregon_Trail">Oregon Trail</a>.</li>
<li>1816 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Otto von Kotzebue" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_von_Kotzebue">Otto von Kotzebue</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Kotzebue Sound" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kotzebue_Sound">Kotzebue Sound</a>.</li>
<li>1819 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="William Smith (mariner)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Smith_(mariner)">William Smith</a>&nbsp;discovers the&nbsp;<a title="South Shetland Islands" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands">South Shetland Islands</a>.</li>

<li>1820 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Edward Bransfield" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Bransfield">Edward Bransfield</a>&nbsp;sights the&nbsp;<a title="Antarctic Peninsula" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Peninsula">Antarctic Peninsula</a>; also discovers northernmost islands of the South Shetlands.</li>
<li>1820&ndash;21 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fabian_Gottlieb_von_Bellingshausen">Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen</a>&nbsp;discovers the northernmost islands of the South Sandwich group; following year discovers&nbsp;<a title="Peter I Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_I_Island">Peter I</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Alexander Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Island">Alexander Islands</a>.</li>
<li>1821 &ndash; English naval officer&nbsp;<a title="John Franklin" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Franklin">John Franklin</a>&nbsp;explores over 800 kilometres (500&nbsp;mi) of coastline from the mouth of the Coppermine River to Point Turnagain on the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Kent Peninsula" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kent_Peninsula">Kent Peninsula</a>.</li>
<li>1821 &ndash; Sealers&nbsp;<a title="Nathaniel Palmer" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nathaniel_Palmer">Nathaniel Palmer</a>&nbsp;and George Powell discover "Powell's Islands" (<a title="South Orkney Islands" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Orkney_Islands">South Orkney Islands</a>).</li>

<li>1823 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Dixon Denham" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dixon_Denham">Dixon Denham</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Walter Oudney" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Oudney">Walter Oudney</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Hugh Clapperton" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugh_Clapperton">Hugh Clapperton</a>&nbsp;are the first Europeans to sight&nbsp;<a title="Lake Chad" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Chad">Lake Chad</a>.</li>

<li>1824&ndash;25 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="&Eacute;tienne Provost" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89tienne_Provost">&Eacute;tienne Provost</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Jim Bridger" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Bridger">Jim Bridger</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Peter Skene Ogden" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Skene_Ogden">Peter Skene Ogden</a>&nbsp;independently reach the&nbsp;<a title="Great Salt Lake" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Salt_Lake">Great Salt Lake</a>.</li>

<li>1826 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Frederick William Beechey" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_William_Beechey">Frederick William Beechey</a>&nbsp;charts the Alaskan coastline from Icy Cape to&nbsp;<a title="Point Barrow" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_Barrow">Point Barrow</a>; also discovers&nbsp;<a title="Vanavana" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanavana">Vanavana</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Fangataufa" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fangataufa">Fangataufa</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Ahunui" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahunui">Ahunui</a>&nbsp;in the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Tuamotu archipelago" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuamotu_archipelago">Tuamotu archipelago</a>.</li>

<li>1827 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Jedediah Smith" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jedediah_Smith">Jedediah Smith</a>&nbsp;crosses the Sierra Nevada (via&nbsp;<a title="Ebbetts Pass" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebbetts_Pass">Ebbetts Pass</a>) and the&nbsp;<a title="Great Basin" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Basin">Great Basin</a>.</li>
<li>1828 &ndash; French explorer&nbsp;<a title="Ren&eacute; Cailli&eacute;" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Cailli%C3%A9">Ren&eacute; Cailli&eacute;</a>&nbsp;is the first European to return alive from Timbuktu.</li>
<li>1829&ndash;30 &ndash;&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="John Ross (Arctic explorer)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Ross_(Arctic_explorer)">John Ross</a>&nbsp;discovers "Boothia Felix" (the&nbsp;<a title="Boothia Peninsula" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boothia_Peninsula">Boothia Peninsula</a>); the following year his nephew&nbsp;<a title="James Clark Ross" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Clark_Ross">James Clark Ross</a>&nbsp;crosses its narrow&nbsp;<a title="Isthmus" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isthmus">isthmus</a>&nbsp;and reaches&nbsp;<a title="King William Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_William_Island">King William Island</a>.</li>
<li>1830 &ndash; English explorer&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Richard Lemon Lander" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Lemon_Lander">Richard Lander</a>&nbsp;and his brother&nbsp;<a title="John Lander (explorer)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lander_(explorer)">John</a>&nbsp;descend the&nbsp;<a title="Niger River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niger_River">Niger</a>&nbsp;for more than 643 kilometres (400&nbsp;mi) from&nbsp;<a title="Bussa, Nigeria" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bussa,_Nigeria">Bussa</a>&nbsp;to its mouth.</li>
<li>1831&ndash;32 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="John Biscoe" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Biscoe">John Biscoe</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Enderby Land" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enderby_Land">Enderby Land</a>; following year discovers&nbsp;<a title="Adelaide Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adelaide_Island">Adelaide</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Anvers Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anvers_Island">Anvers</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Biscoe Islands" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biscoe_Islands">Biscoe Islands</a>.</li>
<li>1833 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Andrei Glazunov" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrei_Glazunov">Andrei Glazunov</a>&nbsp;and Semyon Lukin discover the mouth of the&nbsp;<a title="Yukon River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yukon_River">Yukon River</a>.</li>

<li>1838&ndash;40 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Jules Dumont d'Urville" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jules_Dumont_d%27Urville">Jules Dumont d'Urville</a>&nbsp;discovers the&nbsp;<a title="Joinville Island group" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joinville_Island_group">Joinville Island group</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Ad&eacute;lie Land" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ad%C3%A9lie_Land">Ad&eacute;lie Land</a>&nbsp;(138&deg;21' E).</li>

<ul>
<li>1900 &ndash; Peary explores the north coast of Greenland from Kap Washington to Kap Clarence Wyckoff, on the way reaching&nbsp;<a title="Cape Morris Jesup" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Morris_Jesup">Cape Morris Jesup</a>, the most northern point of mainland Greenland.</li>
<li>1902&ndash;04 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Robert Falcon Scott" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Falcon_Scott">Robert Falcon Scott</a>&nbsp;traces the length of the Ross Ice Shelf, discovers the&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Edward VII Peninsula" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_VII_Peninsula">Edward VII Peninsula</a>, reaches about 82&deg;11&rsquo; S (in the process tracing 600 kilometres (370&nbsp;mi) of the west coast of the shelf), crosses the&nbsp;<a title="Transantarctic Mountains" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transantarctic_Mountains">Transantarctic Mountains</a>&nbsp;and discovers the&nbsp;<a title="Antarctic Plateau" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Plateau">Antarctic Plateau</a>, penetrating nearly 240 kilometres (150&nbsp;mi) into it; he is also the first to see the&nbsp;<a title="McMurdo Dry Valleys" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McMurdo_Dry_Valleys">dry valleys</a>&nbsp;of the&nbsp;<a title="Antarctic" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic">Antarctic</a>.</li>
<li>1903&ndash;06 &ndash; Norwegian polar explorer&nbsp;<a title="Roald Amundsen" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roald_Amundsen">Roald Amundsen</a>&nbsp;leads the first expedition to traverse the entire&nbsp;<a title="Northwest Passage" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northwest_Passage">Northwest Passage</a>, in the sloop&nbsp;<em><a title="Gj&oslash;a" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gj%C3%B8a">Gj&oslash;a</a></em>; Godfred Hansen, his second-in-command, charts the east coast of Victoria Island north to Cape Nansen (72&deg;02'N, 104&deg;45'W).</li>
<li>1906&ndash;07 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Ludvig Mylius-Erichsen" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludvig_Mylius-Erichsen">Ludvig Mylius-Erichsen</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Johan Peter Koch" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johan_Peter_Koch">Johan Peter Koch</a>&nbsp;chart the northeast coast of Greenland from Kap Bismarck (76&deg;42' N) to Kap Clarence Wyckoff (82&deg;52' N), discovering&nbsp;<a title="Danmark Fjord" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danmark_Fjord">Danmark Fjord</a>.</li>
<li>1908&ndash;09 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Frederick Cook" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Cook">Frederick Cook</a>&nbsp;and Peary each claim to have reached the&nbsp;<a title="North Pole" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Pole">North Pole</a>&mdash;the former is a fraud, the latter widely doubted.</li>
<li>1910&ndash;11 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Bernhard Hantzsch" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernhard_Hantzsch">Bernhard Hantzsch</a>&nbsp;crosses Baffin Island from Cumberland Sound to the&nbsp;<a title="Koukdjuak River" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koukdjuak_River">Koukdjuak River</a>, exploring the west coast of the island north to 68&deg;45&rsquo;N.</li>
<li>1911&ndash;12 &ndash; Amundsen becomes the first person to reach the&nbsp;<a title="South Pole" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Pole">South Pole</a>. Scott and his team reach the Pole over a month later, all perishing on the return journey.</li>
<li>1913 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Frederick Marshman Bailey" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Marshman_Bailey">Frederick Bailey</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Henry Morshead" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Morshead">Henry Morshead</a>&nbsp;on their&nbsp;<a title="Bailey&ndash;Morshead exploration of Tsangpo Gorge" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bailey%E2%80%93Morshead_exploration_of_Tsangpo_Gorge">exploration of the Tsangpo Gorge</a>&nbsp;discover the route of the&nbsp;<a title="Yarlung Tsangpo" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yarlung_Tsangpo">Yarlung Tsangpo</a>&nbsp;river.</li>
<li>1913&ndash;14 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Boris Vilkitsky" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boris_Vilkitsky">Boris Vilkitsky</a>&nbsp;and Per Novopashennyy discover&nbsp;<a title="Severnaya Zemlya" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Severnaya_Zemlya">Severnaya Zemlya</a>, surveying parts of its eastern coast from&nbsp;<a title="Arctic Cape" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic_Cape">Mys Arkticheskiy</a>&nbsp;to Mys Vaygacha (its southeast point), as well as much of its south coast west to Mys Neupokoyeva.</li>
<li>1915&ndash;17 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Vilhjalmur Stefansson" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vilhjalmur_Stefansson">Vilhjalmur Stefansson</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Brock Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brock_Island">Brock</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Mackenzie King Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mackenzie_King_Island">Mackenzie King</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Borden Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borden_Island">Borden</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Meighen Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meighen_Island">Meighen</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Lougheed Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lougheed_Island">Lougheed Islands</a>; one of his men, Storker T. Storkerson, charts part of the northeast coast of Victoria Island, discovering the Storkerson Peninsula and&nbsp;<a title="Stefansson Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stefansson_Island">Stefansson Island</a>.</li>
<li>1924&ndash;29 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="J. Dewey Soper" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Dewey_Soper">Joseph Dewey Soper</a>&nbsp;explores the interior of Baffin Island before surveying its west coast north to Hantzsch River.</li>
<li>1926 &ndash; Amundsen,&nbsp;<a title="Lincoln Ellsworth" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lincoln_Ellsworth">Lincoln Ellsworth</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Umberto Nobile" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umberto_Nobile">Umberto Nobile</a>&nbsp;in the airship&nbsp;<a title="Norge (airship)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norge_(airship)">Norge</a>&nbsp;are the first definitely known to have sighted the North Pole.</li>
<li>1927 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="George P. Putnam" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_P._Putnam">George P. Putnam</a>&nbsp;charts the north coast of the Foxe Peninsula from Cape Dorchester to&nbsp;<a class="mw-redirect" title="Bowman Bay, Nunavut" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bowman_Bay,_Nunavut">Bowman Bay</a>.</li>
<li>1930&ndash;32 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Georgy Ushakov" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgy_Ushakov">Georgy Ushakov</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Nikolay Urvantsev" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolay_Urvantsev">Nikolay Urvantsev</a>&nbsp;survey the entire coast of Severnaya Zemlya, showing it to be made up of four main islands:&nbsp;<a title="October Revolution Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/October_Revolution_Island">October Revolution</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Komsomolets Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Komsomolets_Island">Komsomolets</a>,&nbsp;<a title="Pioneer Island (Russia)" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pioneer_Island_(Russia)">Pioneer</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Bolshevik Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bolshevik_Island">Bolshevik Islands</a>&mdash;in all surveying some 2,200 kilometres (1,400&nbsp;mi) of coastline and interior.</li>
<li>1932 &ndash; W. A. Poole discovers&nbsp;<a title="Prince Charles Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_Charles_Island">Prince Charles Island</a>.</li>
<li>1934 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Richard E. Byrd" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_E._Byrd">Richard E. Byrd</a>&nbsp;discovers and names&nbsp;<a title="Roosevelt Island, Antarctica" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roosevelt_Island,_Antarctica">Roosevelt Island</a>.</li>
<li>1937&ndash;41 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Thomas Henry Manning" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Henry_Manning">Thomas and Ella Manning</a>&nbsp;map the west coast of Baffin Island from the Hantzsch River to&nbsp;<a title="Steensby Inlet" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steensby_Inlet">Steensby Inlet</a>.</li>
<li>1940 &ndash; Byrd discovers&nbsp;<a title="Thurston Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thurston_Island">Thurston Island</a>, believing it to be a peninsula.</li>
<li>1948 &ndash; E. C. Kerslake charts Prince Charles,&nbsp;<a title="Air Force Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_Force_Island">Air Force</a>, and&nbsp;<a title="Foley Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foley_Island">Foley Islands</a>.</li>
<li>1950 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Maurice Herzog" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurice_Herzog">Maurice Herzog</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Louis Lachenal" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Lachenal">Louis Lachenal</a>&nbsp;of the&nbsp;<a title="1950 French Annapurna expedition" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1950_French_Annapurna_expedition">French Annapurna expedition</a>&nbsp;become the first climbers to reach the summit of an&nbsp;<a title="Eight-thousander" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eight-thousander">8,000-metre</a>&nbsp;peak.</li>
<li>1953 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Edmund Hillary" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edmund_Hillary">Edmund Hillary</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Tenzing Norgay" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenzing_Norgay">Tenzing Norgay</a>&nbsp;are the first to ascend&nbsp;<a title="Mount Everest" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Everest">Mount Everest</a>.</li>
<li>1954 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Lino Lacedelli" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lino_Lacedelli">Lino Lacedelli</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a title="Achille Compagnoni" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achille_Compagnoni">Achille Compagnoni</a>&nbsp;are the first to ascend&nbsp;<a title="K2" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K2">K2</a>&nbsp;on the&nbsp;<a title="1954 Italian Karakoram expedition to K2" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1954_Italian_Karakoram_expedition_to_K2">Italian Karakoram expedition</a>.</li>
<li>1957 &ndash;&nbsp;<a title="Finn Ronne" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finn_Ronne">Finn Ronne</a>&nbsp;discovers&nbsp;<a title="Berkner Island" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkner_Island">Berkner Island</a>.</li>
</ul>
